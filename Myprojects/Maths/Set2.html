5. Write a recipe in about 100 words for a food item that you can cook the best using the given clues.(5)

name of the food......ingredients and amounts.......cooking methods.........

--> Spaghetti Carbonara

i) Ingredients:

ii) 200g spaghetti
iii) 100g pancetta, diced
iv) 2 large eggs
v) 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
vi) 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
vii) 2 cloves garlic, minced
viii) Salt and pepper to taste
ix) 2 tbsp olive oil

Cooking Method:

1. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente, then drain.
2. In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat and cook the pancetta until crispy. Add minced garlic and sauté briefly.
3. In a bowl, beat eggs and mix with grated Pecorino Romano and Parmesan cheese.
4. Add the hot spaghetti to the pancetta and garlic, then remove from heat. Quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture, ensuring the eggs cook with the residual heat but do not scramble.
5. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve immediately.

Enjoy your delicious Spaghetti Carbonara!

8. Write a review of a film or a book that you have recently seen or read in about 200 words. You may talk about characterisation, strengths and weaknesses, and recommendations you want to make. (8)

Shirish Ko Phool (Sajha Prakashan)

I recently read Shirish Ko Phool by the esteemed Nepali author Parijat, published by Sajha Prakashan, and was deeply moved by its poignant storytelling. The novel revolves around the life of a woman named Shirish, whose complex emotional journey and struggles with society are at the heart of the narrative.

The characterization in Shirish Ko Phool is exceptional. Parijat masterfully crafts Shirish as a multifaceted character, reflecting her inner conflicts and societal challenges with great depth. Her portrayal as a strong yet vulnerable figure adds layers to the narrative, making her experiences resonate deeply with readers.

One of the book's notable strengths is its rich, lyrical prose and its exploration of themes like existential angst, societal expectations, and personal identity. The setting and cultural context are vividly depicted, adding authenticity to the narrative.

However, some readers may find the book’s introspective and somewhat melancholic tone a bit overwhelming at times. The pacing can also be slow, which might affect engagement for those who prefer a more dynamic plot.

Overall, Shirish Ko Phool is a profound and reflective read that I recommend for anyone interested in exploring Nepali literature and the intricacies of human emotions and societal pressures.
